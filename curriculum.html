Our Classes & Curriculum

Creative Learning

Creative learning encourages children to explore their imagination, think critically, and express themselves through various activities. At Blooming Minds Playtorium, we integrate hands-on experiences, storytelling, art, and interactive play to foster curiosity and problem-solving skills.

STEM Activities

At Blooming Minds Playtorium, our STEM activities are designed to make learning fun and engaging for young children. Through playful experiments, building projects, and simple problem-solving tasks, kids explore science, technology, engineering, and math in a hands-on way.

Art Exploration

Our art activities help children express emotions and creativity through painting, drawing, and clay modeling. These projects develop fine motor skills, improve hand-eye coordination, and encourage emotional expression, nurturing both artistic and emotional growth.

Dramatic Play

Our early play activities foster emotional expression and social skills through cooperative and pretend play. By creating stories and role-playing, children develop communication, teamwork, and empathy, supporting their emotional growth in a fun, supportive environment.

Blocks and games

Children build eye-hand coordination and bilateral coordination through activities involving shapes and concepts like counting, sorting, matching, and patterning. These exercises improve fine motor skills and boost cognitive abilities like problem-solving and logical thinking.

Sensory Exploration

Manipulating objects and exploring various textures through sensory touch enhances fine motor skills, tactile awareness, and cognitive development. As children draw, paint, and create, they stimulate curiosity, foster creativity, and develop imaginative thinking.

Math and Manipulatives

Involve sorting, counting, ordering, measuring, and adding through hands-on activities. These playful exercises help children grasp mathematical concepts, develop problem-solving skills, and enhance cognitive abilities while building a strong foundation for future learning.

Music and Movements

It is designed to develop body awareness and position in space, along with building gross motor and balance skills. It also provides self-expression, promotes interaction, and helps in learning concepts through songs, bodily movements, and rhythmic activities.

Hands on Skills

Hands-on skills involve activities like pre-writing skills, tracing, copying, cutting, pasting, and crafting. These tasks not only help develop fine motor skills but also promote hand-eye coordination and attention to detail. These skills foster problem-solving and creativity in children.
